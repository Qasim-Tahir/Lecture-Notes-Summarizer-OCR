# Introduction to Entrepreneurship
## Overview / Objectives
This chapter introduces the concept of entrepreneurship, its importance, and the characteristics of successful entrepreneurs. The learning objectives include:

1. Describe entrepreneurship, corporate entrepreneurship, and the characteristics of entrepreneurial firms.
2. Discuss three main reasons people decide to become entrepreneurs.
3. Identify four main characteristics of successful entrepreneurs.
4. Explain five common myths regarding entrepreneurship.
5. Describe the three types of start-up firms.
6. Discuss the changing demographics of entrepreneurs in the United States.
7. Discuss the positive effects of entrepreneurship and entrepreneurial firms on economies and societies.
8. Explain the entrepreneurial process.

## Key Concepts
* **Entrepreneurship**: The process by which individuals pursue opportunities without regard to resources they currently control for the purpose of exploiting future goods and services.
* **Corporate Entrepreneurship**: The practice of encouraging innovation and risk-taking within an existing firm.
* **Entrepreneurial Intensity**: A firm's position on a continuum ranging from highly conservative to highly entrepreneurial.

## Detailed Explanation
### Why Do People Become Entrepreneurs?
The three primary reasons people become entrepreneurs are:
1. **To be their own boss**: Many entrepreneurs want to own their own firm or have become frustrated working in traditional jobs.
2. **To pursue their own ideas**: Some people are naturally alert and want to see their ideas realized.
3. **To pursue financial rewards**: While not the primary motivation, financial rewards can be a motivator, especially the upside potential.

### Characteristics of Successful Entrepreneurs
The four primary characteristics of successful entrepreneurs are:
1. **Passion for the business**: A strong passion for their business, which gets them through tough times.
2. **Product/Customer Focus**: A focus on providing value to customers and creating products that meet their needs.
3. **Long-term commitment**: A willingness to work hard for an extended period of time.
4. **Ability to overcome setbacks**: The ability to persevere through challenges and setbacks.

## Examples
* **Fraser Doherty**: The founder of SuperJam, who turned his passion for jam-making into a successful business.
* **Kirsten Blowers**: The owner of Riffraff, who is passionate about her business and focuses on providing value to her customers.
* **Marco Zappacosta**: The founder of Thumbtack, who created a platform to connect people with local service providers.

## Summary / Takeaways
* Entrepreneurship is a vital part of the economy, with many people starting their own businesses to pursue their passions and ideas.
* Successful entrepreneurs share certain characteristics, including passion, product/customer focus, long-term commitment, and the ability to overcome setbacks.
* Understanding the entrepreneurial process and the characteristics of successful entrepreneurs can facilitate career success.
