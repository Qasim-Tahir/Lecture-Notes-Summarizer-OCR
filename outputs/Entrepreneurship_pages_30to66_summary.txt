# Entrepreneurship and Entrepreneurial Firms
## Overview / Objectives
Entrepreneurship is the process of creating something new, which is central to the entrepreneurial process. It involves innovation, risk-taking, and a proactive approach. The objectives of this chapter are to:
1. Describe entrepreneurship, corporate entrepreneurship, and the characteristics of entrepreneurial firms.
2. Discuss three main reasons people decide to become entrepreneurs.
3. Identify four main characteristics of successful entrepreneurs.
4. Explain five common myths regarding entrepreneurship.

## What Is Entrepreneurship and Why Is It Important?
Entrepreneurship is defined as the process by which individuals pursue opportunities without regard to resources they currently control for the purpose of exploiting future goods and services. It is about identifying opportunities and putting useful ideas into practice.

## Types of Entrepreneurs
- **Inventor**: Creates something new.
- **Entrepreneur**: Assembles and integrates resources to transform an invention into a viable business.

## Reasons for Becoming an Entrepreneur
1. **Be Their Own Boss**: Desire for independence and autonomy.
2. **Pursue Their Own Ideas**: Passion for an idea and wanting to see it realized.
3. **Pursue Financial Rewards**: Potential for financial gain, although not always the primary motivator.

## Characteristics of Successful Entrepreneurs
1. **Passion for the Business**: A strong belief in and enthusiasm for their business.
2. **Product/Customer Focus**: Understanding the needs of their customers and creating value for them.
3. **Execution Intelligence**: The ability to execute a business plan and adapt to changing circumstances.
4. **Tenacity Despite Failure**: Perseverance and resilience in the face of obstacles and setbacks.

## Common Myths About Entrepreneurs
1. **Entrepreneurs Are Born, Not Made**: Entrepreneurs are not genetically predisposed to be entrepreneurs; their skills and traits can be developed over time.
2. **Entrepreneurs Are Gamblers**: Entrepreneurs are moderate risk-takers, not gamblers.
3. **Entrepreneurs Are Motivated Primarily by Money**: While financial rewards are important, they are not the primary motivator for most entrepreneurs.
4. **Entrepreneurs Should Be Young and Energetic**: Entrepreneurial activity is fairly evenly spread out over age ranges.
5. **Entrepreneurs Love the Spotlight**: Many entrepreneurs avoid public attention and are not motivated by a desire for fame.

## Types of Start-Up Firms
1. **Salary-Substitute Firms**: Small firms that provide a level of income similar to what the owner would earn as an employee.
2. **Lifestyle Firms**: Firms that provide their owners with the opportunity to pursue a particular lifestyle and earn a living.
3. **Entrepreneurial Firms**: Firms that bring new products and services to market by creating and seizing opportunities.

## The Entrepreneurial Process
The entrepreneurial process consists of four steps:
1. **Decision to Become an Entrepreneur**: A triggering event prompts an individual to become an entrepreneur.
2. **Developing Successful Business Ideas**: Recognizing opportunities, generating ideas, feasibility analysis, and developing a business model.
3. **Moving from an Idea to an Entrepreneurial Firm**: Preparing a proper ethical and legal foundation, assessing financial strength and viability, building a new-venture team, and securing funding.
4. **Managing and Growing an Entrepreneurial Firm**: Managing and growing the firm, including marketing, finance, and human resources.

## Positive Effects of Entrepreneurship
Entrepreneurship has a positive impact on economies and societies, including:
- **Innovation**: Creation of new products, services, and processes.
- **Job Creation**: Entrepreneurial firms create new jobs and stimulate economic growth.
- **Improved Quality of Life**: Entrepreneurial firms provide new products and services that improve people's lives.

## Changing Demographics of Entrepreneurs
The demographics of entrepreneurs are changing, with more:
- **Women**: Women are increasingly becoming entrepreneurs, with 11.3 million women-owned businesses in the United States.
- **Minorities**: Minorities are also becoming entrepreneurs, with 8 million minority-owned firms in the United States.
- **Seniors**: Seniors are starting businesses, with 5.4% of individuals aged 62 and older starting a business in 2015.
- **Millennials**: Millennials are expressing interest in entrepreneurship, but only 3.6% of businesses in the United States are owned by someone under 30.
